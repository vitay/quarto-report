The Basal Ganglia (BG) are a set of nuclei located in the basal
forebrain, receiving inputs mostly from the cerebral cortex and
projecting to various motor centers, as well as back to the cortex
through the thalamus, forming a closed-loop. It is involved in major
functions such as reinforcement learning, habit formation, planning and
motor control, but also in diseases such as Parkinson's disease or
Tourette syndrome.
